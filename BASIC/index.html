Quotes

In The Discovery of Heaven, Hedderman's poems move like meditation as he considers those things in his life worth pausing for, worth his contemplation. Juxtaposing the mythological and the literary against the concrete images of the everyday—of horses and wolves, stones and avocados, birches and even television—he creates his own mythology, a world where his words are strong and beautiful, and his stunning imagery is rich with detail and music. The reader is taken to a place he didn't expect to go, a place I want to return to—often.

Karla Huston, Wisconsin Poet Laureate (2017-2018)

Each poem in Choosing a Stone offers both the simplicity and the intricacy of natural things Richard Hedderman is drawn to, such as stones, owls, wolves, snow, ice, flowers, and birches. His brilliant writing is both concise and concentric, like that steelhead he sees caught and released, revealing nature's glint and muscle, a rare moment, ephemeral but here, as the poet shows us what is hidden or not often noticed: the beauty in being. His words always bring me closer to being myself. Questioning an afterlife, the poet see his own heaven as " . . . a place/to lie down in for an hour/with the long summer sun on our faces,/amid the drone of bees in a tended garden. . . I am reminded of Thoreau's attention to the minute details and elevation of the natural world, which gives our lives meaning, and his belief that a life well-lived is one that chooses only what is essential.

Nancy Takacs, author of The Worrier, winner of the Juniper Prize for Poetry

Biography

Richard Hedderman is a poet, essayist, and educator living in Milwaukee, WI. He earned undergraduate degrees in English and Theater at the University of San Francisco, and a Master's Degree in Creative Writing from the University of New Hampshire. He taught English at the College St. Francois-Xavier in Vannes, France.

He is the author of a poetry collection, The Discovery of Heaven (Parallel Press), and his work has appeared in dozens of literary publications both in the U.S. and abroad, including Rattle, South Dakota Review, CutBank, Chautauqua Literary Review, Eclipse, Chicago Quarterly Review, Kestrel, Blue Collar Review, Arsenic Lobster, Saranac Review, The Midwest Quarterly, and Skald (Wales), among many others, and his poetry and prose have been featured on public radio.

His poem series based on Hamlet was published in the anthology In a Fine Frenzy: Poets Respond to Shakespeare (University of Iowa Press). Many of his poems have been anthologized in several books including The Anthology of New England Writers, Best of Arsenic Lobster's 2010, and Feast of Fools, and The Rhysling Anthology Best Science Fiction, Fantasy & Horror Poetry of 2015.

As a museum educator, he has served many years on the staff of the Milwaukee Public Museum as the Education Programs Coordinator and official poet, directing creative writing programming and coordinating the Museum's annual poetry competition for Wisconsin students. He specializes in designing programming in history, archaeology, and anthropology topics, with a special focus on ancient Egypt and mummies.

A veteran theater professional, he is also a Certified Advanced Actor/Combatant with the Society of American Fight Directors, and has taught stage combat and choreographed staged violence in colleges, universities and LORT theaters around the country.
